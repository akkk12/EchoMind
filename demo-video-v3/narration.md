# EchoMind Demo Narration

## 1. The problem

Have you ever opened a project and thought... why did we build it this way? You check Slack. Then Jira. Then an old incident report. And somehow, you still don't have the full answer. That's why I built EchoMind.

## 2. Ask naturally

EchoMind connects those scattered records into one company memory. So, if I've just joined FreshCart's checkout team, I can simply ask: why did checkout freeze during the holiday sale? No incident number. No hunting through channels. Just the question.

## 3. Trust the answer

Here's where it gets interesting. EchoMind gives me an answer, but it also shows the evidence behind it. I can see the confidence, the connected records, and the path from incident to decision. And when I want to verify something, I can open the original source right away.

## 4. Find the right person

Sometimes I don't need another document. I need the person who can help. EchoMind finds experts from the work they actually did: tickets, pull requests, reviews, and discussions. That's much more useful than guessing from a job title.

## 5. Rebuild the timeline

I can also ask how a project evolved. EchoMind rebuilds the timeline from incidents, decisions, tickets, and pull requests. The story was already there. It was just scattered across tools.

## 6. Explore company memory

And when I don't know what to ask yet, I can explore Company Memory. Projects, people, incidents, and decisions are all connected. This is my favorite view, because it shows how the company actually thinks.

## 7. Closing

EchoMind finds the evidence before AI explains it. So answers stay grounded, traceable, and easy to check. Companies already have this knowledge. EchoMind makes sure it doesn't disappear.
