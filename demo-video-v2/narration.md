# EchoMind Founder Demo Narration

## 1. The problem

Have you ever opened a project, seen a strange technical decision, and thought, "Why did we build it this way?" Then someone sends you an old Slack thread, which links to a Jira ticket, which mentions an incident. Twenty minutes later, you still don't have the answer. That's why I built EchoMind.

## 2. Ask and understand

EchoMind connects those scattered company records into one memory. So let's say I've just joined FreshCart's checkout team. I can simply ask, "Why did checkout freeze during the holiday sale?" I don't need an incident number, or the right Slack channel. I just ask the question.

## 3. Evidence, not guesswork

Now here's where things get interesting. EchoMind explains what happened, but it also shows its confidence, the evidence it found, and the chain of events behind the answer. Every citation is clickable. So I'm not being asked to blindly trust an AI response. I can check the original incident immediately.

## 4. Find the right person

Sometimes an explanation isn't enough. You need the person who can actually help. I can ask who knows StockSync inventory reservations, and EchoMind looks at real work: tickets, pull requests, architecture reviews, and discussions. It's expertise based on contribution, not just a job title.

## 5. Reconstruct the story

We can also zoom out and ask how CheckoutFlow evolved. EchoMind connects the original incident to the decisions and implementation work that followed. What surprised me was how much of a project's story already exists inside company tools. The hard part is seeing the whole story at once.

## 6. Explore company memory

And sometimes, you don't know the right question yet. That's why there's Company Memory. I can explore projects, people, incidents, and decisions, then move through the connections between them. This is my favorite view, because it feels less like searching documents and more like exploring how the company actually thinks.

## 7. Closing

Behind the scenes, EchoMind finds the evidence before AI explains it. That keeps every answer grounded and traceable. Companies already have these stories. EchoMind makes sure they don't disappear. It helps teams understand why decisions were made, find the right people, and make better decisions next time.
